## What Is Agentic Commerce?

Agentic commerce describes systems where software agents act on behalf of people or businesses to complete commercial outcomes.

Instead of browsing manually, comparing options, and entering payment details, a user delegates:

- Constraints  
- Preferences  
- Budget  
- Timeline  

An agent:
- Searches structured systems  
- Compares options  
- Negotiates where permitted  
- Executes transactions  
- Confirms settlement  

The defining characteristic is execution, not information.

---

## Why This Matters

The internet historically optimized for attention.

Agentic systems optimize for outcomes.

This shift changes:
- Incentives  
- Cost structures  
- Time efficiency  
- Trust requirements

## Trust Is Structural

Agentic commerce requires:

- Verifiable identity  
- Scoped authorization  
- Traceable execution  
- Revocation capability  

Without trust, autonomous execution is unsafe.

Trust is implemented at the protocol layer, not at the interface layer.
